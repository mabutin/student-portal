BRIEF HISTORY OF OUR LADY OF LOURDES COLLEGE

Our lady of Lourdes College (OLLC) is a private school in Valenzuela City, founded by Mr. Alfredo Demetillo. It was originally conceived by Mr. and Mrs. Alfredo Demetillo in 1986 and continues to be service oriented fulfilling its mission to serve the growing population of Valenzuela City and the neighboring communities. The college admission was structured under the leadership of Mr. Alfredo Demetillo as president of the Board of Trustees. He is ably assisted by the directress (Mrs. Adelia C.     The college site is located at Gen T. De Leon, Valenzuela City. Standing on a two (2) hectare land. The pre-school was officially established in 1986 bearing the name cherub learning center with a modest enrollement of 17 kindergarten students. The grade school started in 1987 with 222 student from nursery to grade 3. Its is significant to note that the founder considered it important to change the name cherub learning center to Our Lady of Lourdes College. In 1989 the school was granted a permit to operate the whole elementary course. At the end of that year twenty graders become the first graduates. Four Years Later, 88 students a marched up a makeshift stage to receive their high school diplomas from Mr. alfredo Demetillo.
    They are distinguished as batch 88. In just a few years this learning has grown far beyond what originally envisioned as the grade school has grown to a phenomenonal 1,147 enrollees while the high school has an enrollment of 1,129 fortifies with sheer courage and with an unfailing faith in the divine providence. Mr. and Mrs alfredo demetillo formally opened the college department in 1994. Course offered were bachelor of science in elementary education, Bachelor of science in secondary education, bachelor of science in business administration and bachelor of science in accountancy. Twenty nine (29) pioneering student enrolled during the first semester of June 1994. With the establishment of the admission and AID office supported by an aggressive recruitment campaign, led by the Vice president for planning and development, Mr Enrico Demetillo, they enrollement steadily grew.
